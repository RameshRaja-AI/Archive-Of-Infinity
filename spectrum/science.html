Physics

Physics is the natural science that studies matter, its fundamental constituents, its motion and behavior through space and time, and the related entities of energy and force. Physics is one of the most fundamental scientific disciplines, with its main goal being to understand how the universe behaves.

Classical Mechanics

Classical mechanics describes the motion of macroscopic objects, from projectiles to parts of machinery, and astronomical objects, such as spacecraft, planets, stars, and galaxies.

  • Newton's Laws of Motion
  • Conservation of Energy and Momentum
  • Rotational Dynamics
  • Harmonic Motion

Thermodynamics

Thermodynamics studies the effects of changes in temperature, pressure, and volume on physical systems at the macroscopic scale by analyzing the collective motion of their particles using statistics.

  • Laws of Thermodynamics
  • Heat Transfer
  • Entropy and Disorder
  • Thermal Equilibrium

Electromagnetism

Electromagnetism is a branch of physics involving the study of the electromagnetic force, a type of physical interaction that occurs between electrically charged particles.

  • Electric Fields and Charges
  • Magnetic Fields and Forces
  • Electromagnetic Waves
  • Maxwell's Equations

Quantum Mechanics

Quantum mechanics is a fundamental theory in physics that provides a description of the physical properties of nature at the scale of atoms and subatomic particles.

  • Wave-Particle Duality
  • Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle
  • Quantum Entanglement
  • Quantum Field Theory

Relativity

The theory of relativity usually encompasses two interrelated theories by Albert Einstein: special relativity and general relativity.

  • Special Relativity: Time Dilation and Length Contraction
  • General Relativity: Gravity as Spacetime Curvature
  • Relativistic Effects on Time and Space
  • Black Holes and Gravitational Waves

Chemistry

Chemistry is the scientific study of the properties and behavior of matter. It is a physical science that covers the elements that make up matter to the compounds made of atoms, molecules and ions: their composition, structure, properties, behavior and the changes they undergo during a reaction with other substances.

Atomic Structure

Atomic structure refers to the structure of an atom comprising a nucleus (center) in which the protons (positively charged) and neutrons (neutral) are present. The negatively charged particles called electrons revolve around the center of the nucleus.

  • Atomic Models: Bohr, Quantum Mechanical
  • Electron Configuration
  • Periodic Trends
  • Isotopes and Nuclear Chemistry

Chemical Bonding

Chemical bonding is the formation of a chemical bond between two or more atoms, molecules, or ions to give rise to a chemical compound. These chemical bonds are what keep atoms together in molecules.

  • Ionic Bonding
  • Covalent Bonding
  • Metallic Bonding
  • Intermolecular Forces

Organic Chemistry

Organic chemistry is the study of the structure, properties, composition, reactions, and preparation of carbon-containing compounds.

  • Hydrocarbons and Functional Groups
  • Organic Reactions and Mechanisms
  • Stereochemistry
  • Biomolecules: Proteins, Lipids, Carbohydrates

Biochemistry

Biochemistry is the branch of science that explores the chemical processes within and related to living organisms. It is a laboratory-based science that brings together biology and chemistry.

  • Enzymes and Metabolism
  • DNA, RNA and Protein Synthesis
  • Cellular Respiration
  • Photosynthesis

Analytical Chemistry

Analytical chemistry studies and uses instruments and methods to separate, identify, and quantify matter. It involves the study of the chemical composition of materials.

  • Spectroscopy Methods
  • Chromatography Techniques
  • Mass Spectrometry
  • Electrochemical Analysis

Biology

Biology is the natural science that studies life and living organisms, including their physical structure, chemical processes, molecular interactions, physiological mechanisms, development, and evolution.

Cell Biology

Cell biology is the study of cell structure and function, and it revolves around the concept that the cell is the fundamental unit of life.

  • Cell Structure and Organelles
  • Cell Membrane and Transport
  • Cell Division: Mitosis and Meiosis
  • Cellular Respiration and Energy Production

Genetics

Genetics is the study of genes, genetic variation, and heredity in organisms. It is an important branch in biology because heredity is vital to organisms' evolution.

  • DNA Structure and Replication
  • Gene Expression: Transcription and Translation
  • Mendelian Genetics and Inheritance Patterns
  • Genetic Engineering and Biotechnology

Evolution

Evolution is the process by which different kinds of living organisms developed and diversified from earlier forms during the history of the Earth.

  • Natural Selection and Adaptation
  • Speciation and Biodiversity
  • Evolutionary History of Life
  • Human Evolution

Ecology

Ecology is the study of the relationships between living organisms, including humans, and their physical environment.

  • Ecosystems and Biomes
  • Population Dynamics
  • Food Webs and Energy Flow
  • Environmental Conservation

Physiology

Physiology is the scientific study of functions and mechanisms in a living system.

  • Nervous System and Brain Function
  • Circulatory and Respiratory Systems
  • Immune System and Disease Defense
  • Hormones and Endocrine Regulation

Mathematics

Mathematics is the science of structure, order, and relation that has evolved from counting, measuring, and describing the shapes of objects. It deals with logical reasoning and quantitative calculation.

Algebra

Algebra is a branch of mathematics dealing with symbols and the rules for manipulating these symbols. It is central to all areas of mathematics.

  • Equations and Inequalities
  • Functions and Their Graphs
  • Polynomials and Rational Expressions
  • Linear Algebra and Matrices

Calculus

Calculus is the mathematical study of continuous change, in the same way that geometry is the study of shape and algebra is the study of generalizations of arithmetic operations.

  • Limits and Continuity
  • Differentiation: Rates of Change
  • Integration: Area and Accumulation
  • Differential Equations

Geometry

Geometry is a branch of mathematics that studies the sizes, shapes, positions, and dimensions of things.

  • Euclidean Geometry
  • Coordinate Geometry
  • Trigonometry
  • Non-Euclidean Geometries

Statistics and Probability

Statistics is the discipline that concerns the collection, organization, analysis, interpretation, and presentation of data. Probability is the branch of mathematics concerning numerical descriptions of how likely an event is to occur.

  • Descriptive Statistics
  • Probability Distributions
  • Hypothesis Testing
  • Regression Analysis

Number Theory

Number theory is a branch of pure mathematics devoted primarily to the study of the integers and integer-valued functions.

  • Prime Numbers
  • Divisibility and Modular Arithmetic
  • Diophantine Equations
  • Cryptography Applications

Astronomy

Astronomy is the study of everything in the universe beyond Earth's atmosphere. That includes objects we can see with our naked eyes, like the Sun, the Moon, the planets, and the stars. It also includes objects we can only see with telescopes or other instruments, like distant galaxies and tiny particles.

The Solar System

Our Solar System consists of the Sun and the objects that orbit it, either directly or indirectly.

  • The Sun: Our Star
  • Inner Planets: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars
  • Outer Planets: Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune
  • Dwarf Planets, Asteroids, and Comets

Stars and Stellar Evolution

Stars are massive, luminous spheres of plasma held together by their own gravity. The nearest star to Earth is the Sun.

  • Stellar Evolution: From Protostars to Supernovae
  • Neutron Stars, Pulsars, and Black Holes

A galaxy is a system of millions or billions of stars, together with gas and dust, held together by gravitational attraction.

  • Galaxy Formation and Evolution
  • Galaxy Clusters and Superclusters

Cosmology is the study of the origin, evolution, and eventual fate of the universe.

  • The Expanding Universe and Its Future

Earth Sciences

Earth science or geoscience includes all fields of natural science related to the planet Earth. This is a branch of science dealing with the physical, chemical, and biological complex constitutions and synergistic linkages of Earth's four spheres: the biosphere, the hydrosphere, the atmosphere, and the geosphere.

Geology is the study of the Earth, the materials of which it is made, the structure of those materials, and the processes acting upon them.

Meteorology is the scientific study of the atmosphere that focuses on weather processes and forecasting.

  • Water Resources and Management

Environmental science is an interdisciplinary academic field that integrates physical, biological, and information sciences to study the environment and solve environmental problems.
